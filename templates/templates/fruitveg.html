Fruits and Vegetables


Eating enough fruits and vegetables is an important part of a healthy diet.

Fruits and vegetables are…

  • Rich in vitamins and minerals, which helps maintain the normal functioning of different systems in the body. One example is the immune system
  • High in dietary fiber, which can facilitate bowel movements and lower blood cholesterol.
  • Low in calories. Eating a lot of fruits and vegetables will give the feeling of fullness and help with weight control.

When adding fruits and vegetables to your diet, you need to pay attention to both the quantity and quality.

Quantity Quality
Consume at least 5 servings of fruits and vegetables per day Consume at least 3 different colors of fruits and vegetables per day
One serving of fruit and vegetables is…
  • 1 cereal bowl of salad greens
  • About 5 broccoli florets
  • 1 bowl of raw vegetables (such as carrot, mushroom, zucchini)
  • ½ bowl of cooked vegetables
  • 1 medium sized tomato
 Vegetables of different colors are rich in different types of anti-oxidants, for example:
  • Red - Lycopene
  • Orange and yellow - Carotene
  • Green - Chlorophyll
  • Purple - Resveratrol

Tips to eat more fruit and vegetables:

  • Add fruits to your breakfast. For example: add dried fruits to oatmeal or have fresh fruits and yogurt for breakfast.
  • Have a fruit for tea or dessert.
  • When you eat in a restaurant, add a vegetable side dish.
  • When you cook at home, at least cook 5oz/150g of vegetables for each family member.
  • Use meat as condiments to add taste to your vegetables. For example: add ground meat to stir-fry vegetables.
  • Include one green vegetable and one colored vegetables in each meal. Example of colored vegetables: tomatoes, onions, eggplants, carrots, sweet bell peppers, etc.
