Dietary Recommendations for Cancer Prevention



Research finds that a healthy lifestyle can lower the risk of cancer recurrence and the risk of new cancer. Having a healthy lifestyle has many other benefits, such as lowering the risk of cardiovascular diseases, hypertension and diabetes. It is very important in maintaining health.

Although there are no special diet or food that can cause or cure cancer, a balanced diet and healthy weight can help lower the risk of cancer recurrence.

The World Cancer Research Fund and American Cancer Society published dietary guidelines for cancer prevention. Their content are similar, main points as followed:

  1. Maintain a healthy weight with diet and exercise. If you are overweight now, any reduction in weight will benefit your health.
  2. Consume at least 5 servings of fruits and vegetables each day. One serving is one medium sized fruit or half a bowl of cooked vegetables.
  3. Choose whole grain products whenever possible, such as whole grain bread and brown rice, and not refined products, such as white bread and white rice.
  4. Do not eat too much red meat, including pork, beef, and lamb. Do not consume red meat for more than 2-3 times per week. Choose fish, poultry or plant based protein (including legumes and soy products, such as tofu).
  5. Avoid eating processed meat whenever possible, including sausages, ham and canned meat.
  6. Limit intake of foods and drinks with added sugar, such as sodas, sweetened tea and lemonade.
  7. Choose low sodium foods and limit added salt.
  8. Drink plenty (at least 8 cups) of water each day
  9. Avoid drinking alcohol. If you decide to drink occasionally, limit drinking to no more than once per day and no more than 1-2 glasses each time.
